Introduction

  • Businesses invest heavily in marketing to acquire customers and drive revenue.

  • This report analyzes customer acquisition data to understand marketing effectiveness across customer segments.

  • Understanding these patterns helps optimize marketing spend allocation.

  • The analysis uses real customer data from 2024-2026 to provide actionable insights.

Research Question

Q)How does marketing spend effectiveness (purchase amount per dollar spent) vary across customer segments (Consumer, SMB, Enterprise)?

This question examines ROI differences between segments to guide budget allocation. Higher effectiveness segments should receive priority marketing investment. The analysis uses purchase-to-spend ratios and visualization of key metrics.

Dataset Introduction

This dataset tracks customer acquisition metrics from 2024-2026 across three segments: Consumer, SMB, and Enterprise. It records signup dates, marketing spend, website visits, purchase amounts, and customer notes for 150 customers. The data shows varied marketing outcomes with missing values in dates and purchases indicating incomplete tracking.

Data cleaning process: Standardized multiple date formats using lubridate functions, converted character numeric columns to proper types, removed fully incomplete rows, and eliminated duplicates while preserving data integrity.

Reading and Filtering Dataset

Dataset Introduction

This dataset records customer-level marketing and purchasing behaviour across three business segments: Consumer, SMB (Small-to-Medium Business), and Enterprise.

Each observation represents a single customer and includes the marketing dollars spent to engage them, the number of site visits they made, and their total purchase amount.

The central research question is: How does marketing spend effectiveness — measured as purchase amount per dollar of marketing spend — vary across customer segments?

This question is motivated by the need to guide budget allocation decisions, since segments with a higher return on marketing investment should receive priority funding.

The analysis derives a per-customer ROI ratio and compares its distribution and drivers across the three segments.

The dataset was cleaned by removing the near-empty notes column, converting signup_date to a proper date type, and setting segment as an ordered factor. Rows missing purchase_amount were excluded from all analyses, and effectiveness (purchase amount ÷ marketing spend) was computed as the key derived metric.

Dataset Description

The cleaned data set contains 150observations and 6variables. The variables are: customer_id , signup_date , marketing_spend, visits , purchase_amount , and segment .The character-based customer identifier is the customer_id, a signup date(signup_date with some missing values, numeric fields for marketing spendmarketing_spend), number of visits(visits), and purchase amount(purchase amounts(which also has some missing values), and a three-level categorical segment variable(segment) (Consumer, SMB, and a third unlisted level).
